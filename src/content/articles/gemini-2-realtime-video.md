Google has updated Gemini 2.0 with real-time video understanding capabilities, allowing the model to process and respond to live video streams — not just pre-recorded clips. The feature is available in Gemini Advanced and via the Gemini API.

## How Real-Time Video Works

The model processes video at up to 30 frames per second, maintaining a rolling context window that allows it to answer questions about what is happening in the current frame, reference earlier moments in the stream, and track objects or actions over time.

## Use Cases

Early enterprise users have deployed the capability for quality control in manufacturing, real-time sports analysis, and accessibility applications that describe live environments to visually impaired users.

## Comparison With GPT-4o Vision

GPT-4o also supports video input, but currently processes video as sampled frames rather than a continuous stream. Gemini 2.0's streaming approach provides lower-latency responses for time-sensitive applications.
